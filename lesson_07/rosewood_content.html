Rosewood

Rosewood is a term used for several types of hardwood trees and their wood. The term is applied to various species within the genus Dalbergia, as well as some other genera in the family Fabaceae. Rosewood is prized for its rich, deep red color and its distinctive grain pattern.

There are several species of rosewood, with the most well-known being Dalbergia nigra, commonly known as Brazilian rosewood. This species is highly valued for its use in musical instruments, particularly guitars and violins, due to its excellent acoustic properties.
